OpWell Concierge Clinical Evidence Brief

Presenter: Dr. Ornella Oluwole, MD, D.ABA | POQI-Aligned Surgery School

Executive Summary

OpWell Concierge is an independent, telehealth-based perioperative specialty service for healthcare providers and surgical teams. It translates multicenter RCT evidence from Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS), Perioperative Surgical Home (PSH), and prehabilitation literature into a turnkey referral model that optimizes surgical patients across three phases: preoperative preparation, intraoperative coordination, and structured 30-day post-discharge monitoring.

Critical Differentiator: Structured vs. Passive Monitoring

Key Finding: Boggan et al. (2025, Annals of Internal Medicine) — 7 RCTs, n = 7,075:

Simple telephone follow-up = NO reduction in readmissions or ED visits

OpWell's structured digital platform: OR 0.42–0.67 for readmissions and ED visits

Passive follow-up doesn't work. Structured monitoring does. This is the competitive advantage.

OpWell Surgery School: POQI Consensus Alignment

Every patient completes a 23-module digital preparatory framework aligned with 2025 POQI recommendations:

  1. Procedure-Specific — Personalized paths for 8 major specialties
  2. Behavior Change — Targeted nutritional, fitness, cessation coaching
  3. Psychological — Anxiety mitigation and clinical visualization
  4. Practical Preparation — Home configuration and caregiver alignment
  5. Expectations — Comprehensive 30-day week-by-week recovery roadmap
  6. Monitoring & Triage — Structured digital touchpoints with QoR-15

B2B Integration & Clinical Continuity

OpWell operates as a collaborative extension of your existing clinical practice:

  • Does NOT alter surgical plans
  • Does NOT manage technical operative complications
  • Does NOT replace standard postoperative office visits

By intercepting low-risk programmatic queries, providing objective medical clearance reporting, and delivering a validated Readiness Score, OpWell ensures patients arrive optimized, compliant, and structurally prepared for anesthesia and surgery.

Transparency on Evidence Certainty

STRONGEST (Moderate-High): ERAS pathways consistently reduce LOS and complications across high-quality RCTs.

STRONG (Very Low to Moderate): Prehabilitation shows 50–54% complication reduction; results robust after high risk-of-bias exclusion.

MODERATE (Low-Moderate): Post-discharge monitoring works best with structured digital platforms, NOT passive calls.

FOUNDATIONAL (Low): PSH model lacks RCTs; limited to before-and-after cohort data.

Bottom Line: This brief proactively addresses evidence limitations. Rigor beats oversimplification.
