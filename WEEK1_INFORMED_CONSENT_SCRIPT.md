## SCRIPT (60 seconds)

### OPENING [0-3 sec] — Hook
**[Direct to camera, serious but warm tone]**

"I'm an anesthesiologist. I meet surgical patients 10 minutes before surgery. And every single day, I ask them one question: 'What surgery are you having today?'"

**[Pause 1 second for impact]**

---

### PROBLEM [3-12 sec] — Building tension
**[Slightly closer to camera, emphasis on the problem]**

"You know what most people say? 'Um... I'm not sure. Something on my... abdomen?' 

Or: 'My surgeon said it was important, but I didn't really understand.'

And my personal favorite: *[slight head shake]* 'I have no idea.'"

---

### INSIGHT [12-25 sec] — The revelation
**[Step back, broader perspective]**

"This isn't a surgeon problem. This is a system problem. 

Your surgeon explains things at a surgeon's level. You don't have time to process. You sign a consent form you barely read. 

And suddenly you're on my operating table, and you have no idea what's about to happen."

---

### SOLUTION [25-40 sec] — What OpWell does
**[Confident, empowering tone]**

"That's why I started OpWell Concierge. 

Two to six weeks before surgery, we sit down. One-on-one. I explain YOUR procedure—in YOUR language. We talk about risks, recovery, what to expect.

You ask questions. Real questions. Questions that matter to you."

---

### OUTCOME [40-55 sec] — Why it matters
**[Warm, reassuring]**

"When you walk into that OR, you're not confused. You're prepared. You're confident. And you understand what's happening to your body.

That changes everything."

---

### CTA [55-60 sec] — Call to action
**[Direct to camera, authentic smile]**

"Surgery is personal. Your preparation should be, too. 

Link in bio to schedule your pre-op consultation."

## CAPCUT PRODUCTION GUIDE

### VIDEO BREAKDOWN (What to Film)

#### Shot 1: Direct-to-Camera (0-3 sec) — THE HOOK
- **What**: You, close-up, looking directly at camera
- **Where**: Clinic workspace, professional background (not your bedroom)
- **Lighting**: Natural window light or ring light (clean, professional)
- **Tone**: Serious, authoritative, but not scary
- **Length to film**: 8-10 seconds (you'll trim to 3 sec)
- **Recording tip**: Pause 1 full second after the hook—let it sink in

---

#### Shot 2: Medium Shot + B-Roll (3-12 sec) — THE PROBLEM
- **What**: You speaking naturally (slightly loose, more conversational) + quick B-roll clips
- **B-roll options**:
  - Desk with surgical paperwork/consent forms (quick clip)
  - Your OR workspace (monitors, equipment—quick pan)
  - Notebook/clipboard (representing documentation)
  - Close-up of consent form being signed (hands only, no patient faces)
- **Where**: Clinic or OR (safe, HIPAA-compliant angles)
- **Lighting**: Same as Shot 1
- **Length to film**: 15-20 seconds (you'll trim to 9 sec)
- **Tone**: Empathetic but highlighting the absurdity

---

#### Shot 3: Wider Shot (12-25 sec) — THE INSIGHT
- **What**: You, step back from camera (medium shot), explaining the system problem
- **Where**: Standing in clinic space, arms open slightly (not stiff)
- **B-roll options** (optional):
  - Fast-motion of clipboard/pen
  - Blurred image of OR lights (symbolic: the scary unknown)
  - Clock ticking down (representing limited pre-op time)
- **Lighting**: Same professional setup
- **Length to film**: 18-22 seconds (trim to 13 sec)
- **Tone**: Thoughtful, problem-solver energy

---

#### Shot 4: Confident Close-Up (25-40 sec) — THE SOLUTION
- **What**: You, close to camera again, speaking with confidence
- **Where**: Clinic workspace (same as Shot 1)
- **B-roll options**:
  - Hands holding a tablet or notebook (representing 1-on-1 conversation)
  - Calendar showing "6 weeks before surgery"
  - Patient education materials on desk
  - You gesturing while explaining
- **Lighting**: Consistent with other shots
- **Length to film**: 20-25 seconds (trim to 15 sec)
- **Tone**: Solution-focused, empowering

---

#### Shot 5: Warm, Reassuring (40-55 sec) — THE OUTCOME
- **What**: You, broader view, speaking to the impact
- **Where**: Clinic space, maybe sitting or standing
- **B-roll options** (optional, light):
  - Soft focus of medical environment
  - Close-up of notes/education materials
  - Fading to calm, peaceful imagery (representing confidence)
- **Lighting**: Slightly softer if possible (warm, reassuring)
- **Length to film**: 18-20 seconds (trim to 15 sec)
- **Tone**: Warm, confident, reassuring

---

#### Shot 6: Direct CTA (55-60 sec) — CALL TO ACTION
- **What**: You, close-up, authentic smile, looking at camera
- **Where**: Same clinic workspace as other close-ups
- **B-roll**: OpWell logo fade-in at the very end
- **Lighting**: Consistent, warm
- **Length to film**: 8-10 seconds (trim to 5 sec)
- **Tone**: Genuine, accessible, warm
- **Optional**: End with your name/credentials on screen

---

## TOTAL FILMING TIME

You'll film roughly **90-120 seconds of raw footage** and edit down to 60 seconds.

**Suggested order to film** (for efficiency):
1. All close-ups (Shots 1, 4, 6) — same position, same lighting
2. Medium shots (Shots 2, 3, 5) — walk through clinic, vary positions
3. B-roll clips — quick shots of desk, forms, equipment (10-15 seconds total)

**Time to shoot**: 20-30 minutes for all takes (do 2-3 takes per section)

## FILMING TIPS (For Best Results)

1. **Lighting**: Film near a window for natural light, or use a ring light
2. **Audio**: Use phone's built-in mic, but film in a quiet room (no background noise)
3. **Stability**: Use a phone tripod or prop your phone on something stable
4. **Multiple takes**: Record each section 2-3 times, pick the most natural
5. **Speak naturally**: Don't memorize word-for-word; know your points and speak conversationally
6. **Pause strategically**: Use pauses for emphasis (like after the hook)
7. **Eye contact**: Look directly at the camera lens
8. **Energy**: Start strong with the hook; maintain warmth throughout
