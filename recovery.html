Post-Operative Care

Professional physician-led recovery support when you need it most.

Why Post-Op Care Matters

The days and weeks after surgery are critical — yet most patients are left to navigate recovery alone. Complications can develop silently. Pain management is inadequate. Wound healing stalls. Medication interactions go unnoticed.

OpWell provides physician-led post-operative support within 72 hours of surgery and weekly check-ins for 30 days.

Our Recovery Support Includes

  • Initial post-op check-in within 72 hours of surgery
  • Weekly follow-up consultations for 30 days
  • Pain management optimization & medication adjustments
  • Wound healing assessment & infection monitoring
  • Nutrition & physical activity guidance
  • Early identification of complications (blood clots, seromas, infection)
  • Comorbidity management (diabetes, hypertension, anticoagulation)
  • Mental health & anxiety support coordination

Your Recovery Timeline

Days 1-3 Post-Op

Your initial check-in within 72 hours. We assess pain control, wound status, medication side effects, and any concerning symptoms. This is when early complications are most likely to emerge — and when early intervention prevents bigger problems.

Weeks 1-2

Weekly consultations focus on pain management optimization, nutrition, physical activity readiness, and wound healing. We catch infections early and adjust medications as your body heals.

Weeks 3-4

Final check-in to confirm healing trajectory, clarify activity restrictions, and discuss any lingering concerns as you transition back to normal life.

Who Benefits?

All surgical patients benefit from post-operative support — but especially bariatric surgery patients, cosmetic surgery patients, and anyone with comorbidities (diabetes, hypertension, sleep apnea, obesity) that complicate recovery.

How It Works

  1. Book your post-operative care consultation before or after surgery
  2. Initial check-in within 72 hours of surgery via telehealth
  3. Weekly follow-ups for 30 days (or longer if needed)
  4. Direct access to physician team for complications or urgent questions
