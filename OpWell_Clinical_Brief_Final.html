Executive Summary

OpWell Concierge™ is an independent, telehealth-based perioperative specialty service available through provider partnership referrals and direct-to-consumer patient access. It translates multicenter RCT evidence from Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS), Perioperative Surgical Home (PSH), and prehabilitation literature into a comprehensive optimization model across two distinct phases: preoperative preparation and structured 30-day post-discharge monitoring. OpWell does not participate in intraoperative care. This brief presents the primary evidence base, with exact effect estimates, confidence intervals, and certainty-of-evidence ratings for each clinical claim.

Critical Nuance: Structured vs. Passive Post-Discharge Monitoring

Key Differentiator: Boggan et al. (2025, Annals of Internal Medicine) — systematic review of 7 RCTs, n = 7,075: Simple, single-contact telephone calls within 7 days of discharge produced no reduction in 30-day readmissions (RD 0.00; 95% CI −0.02 to 0.02; moderate certainty) or ED visits.

OpWell's approach: The readmission and ED reductions cited above (OR 0.42–0.67) are specific to structured, technology-enabled, multi-contact monitoring platforms — precisely the model OpWell employs. Passive telephone follow-up is insufficient; structured digital triage is the evidence-based standard. This distinction is a competitive differentiator and should be emphasized in value-analysis discussions.

OpWell Surgery School: POQI Consensus Alignment

Every referred patient completes a 23-module digital preparatory framework structurally aligned with the 2025 Perioperative Quality Initiative (POQI) consensus recommendations across five therapeutic domains:

  1. Procedure-Specific — Personalized pathways for 8 major surgical specialties.
  2. Behavior Change — Targeted nutritional, fitness, and cessation coaching.
  3. Psychological — Anxiety mitigation and clinical visualization.
  4. Practical Preparation — Home configuration and caregiver alignment.
  5. Expectations — Comprehensive 30-day week-by-week recovery roadmap.

B2B Integration and Clinical Continuity

OpWell Concierge operates exclusively in the preoperative and post-discharge phases as a collaborative extension of existing clinical practice. It does not provide intraoperative services, alter surgical plans, manage technical operative complications, or replace standard postoperative office visits.

Preoperative Phase: By intercepting low-risk programmatic queries, providing objective medical clearance reporting, and delivering a validated Readiness Score alongside a physical completion certificate, OpWell ensures patients arrive optimized, compliant, and structurally prepared for the physiologic stress of anesthesia and surgery.

Post-Discharge Phase: OpWell's structured digital monitoring platform triages recovery concerns during the critical 30-day window via technology-enabled multi-contact touchpoints (not passive calls), escalating actionable findings back to the surgical team for clinical integration.

Transparency Statement on Evidence Certainty

Strongest Evidence (Moderate-High Certainty): ERAS pathways consistently reduce hospital LOS by ~1.9 days and complications (RR 0.71) across high-quality RCTs. This represents the gold standard for perioperative optimization.

Strong Evidence (Very Low to Moderate Certainty): Exercise prehabilitation demonstrates consistent 50–54% complication reduction across multiple meta-analyses. Results are graded very low to moderate due to trial-level risk of bias and imprecision, but remain robust after exclusion of high risk-of-bias trials. Confirmatory data from Ramic et al. support the OR range.

Moderate Evidence (Low-Moderate Certainty): Post-discharge monitoring shows the strongest signal from structured, technology-enabled platforms (OR 0.42–0.67 for readmissions and ED visits) rather than passive telephone contact. The Boggan et al. null finding for passive calls reinforces this distinction and is critical for OpWell's competitive positioning.

Foundational Evidence (Low Certainty): The anesthesiologist-led PSH model lacks RCT-level evidence; available data are limited to single-institution before-and-after cohort studies. These limitations are standard for complex health-system interventions and do not negate the consistent direction of effect across all domains.

Bottom Line: OpWell's framework is supported by moderate-to-high certainty evidence in its core domains (ERAS, prehabilitation, structured monitoring). This brief proactively addresses evidence limitations—a hallmark of rigor rather than weakness. Evidence-literate CMOs will appreciate the transparency.
