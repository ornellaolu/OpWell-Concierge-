4 Weeks Before Your Surgery

The months and weeks leading up to surgery are when you have the most control. These 4 weeks are your foundation for a smooth perioperative experience.

  • Schedule your OpWell Concierge anesthesia consultation. Get personalized risk assessment and preparation tailored to your specific surgery and medical history.
  • Begin your surgical health optimization. This includes optimizing nutrition, increasing activity levels (if cleared), and addressing any untreated health conditions.
  • Review all current medications with your primary care doctor. Bring a complete list to your OpWell consultation.
  • Get baseline labs if recommended. Your OpWell consultation will clarify what labs are needed.

2 Weeks Before Surgery

  • Follow pre-op diet recommendations provided by your surgeon and OpWell team.
  • Arrange transportation home. You cannot drive after anesthesia — plan now.
  • Finalize time off work. Ensure you have adequate recovery time scheduled.
  • Confirm all pre-op appointments with your surgeon and anesthesiologist.

1 Week Before Surgery

  • Complete all pre-op testing. Blood work, EKG, imaging — complete it all this week.
  • Review NPO (nothing by mouth) instructions carefully. These are critical for anesthesia safety.
  • Arrange your recovery space. Ensure someone is home with you for the first 24 hours post-op.
  • Prepare recovery medications. Have post-op medications filled and ready.

The Day Before Surgery

  • Follow your surgeon's pre-op instructions exactly. This typically includes NPO after midnight.
  • Avoid alcohol. Alcohol interferes with anesthesia and recovery.
  • Pack a hospital bag if having in-patient surgery.
  • Get 8 hours of sleep. This is non-negotiable for surgical safety.

Surgery Day Checklist

  • Arrive on time. Follow your facility's arrival instructions exactly.
  • Bring required documents: ID, insurance cards, medication list, pre-op lab results.
  • Wear comfortable clothing. No metal, jewelry, or makeup.
  • Bring your support person. Someone should be present for anesthesia discussions.
  • Be honest with the anesthesia team about all medications, supplements, and health conditions.
